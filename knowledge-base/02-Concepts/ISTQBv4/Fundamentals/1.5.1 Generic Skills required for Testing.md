**K2**

There are skills particularly relevant to testers:
1. Testing knowledge (using test techniques)
2. Thoroughness, carefulness, curiosity, attention to details, being methodical (identifying defects, especially the ones that are difficult to find)
3. Good communication skills, active listening, being a team player (effective interaction with stakeholders, convey information to others, understandable, report and discuss defects)
4. Analytical thinking, critical thinking, creativity (increasing effectiveness of testing)
5. Technical knowledge (increasing testing efficiency by using appropriate test tools)
6. Domain knowledge (able to understand and to communicate with end-users/business representatives)

Therefore, **testers are often the bearers of bad news**. This communication skill is crucial for testers. **Communicating test results may be perceived as criticism of the product and of its author.** It can be difficult to accept information that disagrees with currently held beliefs. Testing may be  perceived as a destructive activity, even though it contributes greatly to project success and product quality. To improve this view, **information about defects and failures should and must be communicated in a constructive way.**
